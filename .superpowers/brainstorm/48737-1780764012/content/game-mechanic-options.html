How does "Mafia" map onto a restaurant?

Three ways to inject social deception into the dinner experience

🥷
THE FOODIE IMPOSTOR
One person is secretly lying about their order

Option A: The Impostor

Everyone orders for real... except one. The Impostor secretly picks a DIFFERENT dish but pretends they ordered what they actually got. During the game, players discuss, accuse, and try to identify who's faking.

Restaurant hook: "Someone at this table is lying about what they ordered."

Pros

  • Natural Mafia dynamic — one deceiver, many hunters
  • Performance IS the game — you have to sell your lie
  • Simple to explain in 10 seconds

Cons

  • Only 1 impostor — others are just detectives
  • Small group (3-4) means impostor gets caught fast
🎭
SECRET ROLES
Everyone gets a hidden role that changes how they play

Option B: Secret Roles

After ordering, each player gets a secret role assigned by the AI. Roles change your objective:

  • 🦹 Spy — You MUST convince the table you ordered something you didn't
  • 🔍 Inspector — You get one AI-powered clue about someone's real order
  • 🛡️ Guardian — You can protect one player from being accused
  • 😋 Honest Foodie — You just tell the truth and try to spot the spies

Restaurant hook: "Your order is your identity. Your role is your mission."

Pros

  • EVERYONE has agency — not just the impostor
  • Multiple spies = more chaos and performance
  • AI-generated clues = unique every game

Cons

  • Harder to explain quickly
  • More complex to build (role assignment, special abilities)
🔥
THE BLIND TASTE BET
Everyone lies, everyone hunts, AI is the judge

Option C: Everyone Lies

After ordering, everyone must describe their dish — but you can lie. You can describe your real dish OR describe a different dish entirely. Then the table votes on who they think is lying.

The twist: Chef Marco (AI) gives each player a secret briefing — personalized tips on how to sell your lie or spot others'. It's like having a Mafia moderator in your pocket.

Restaurant hook: "Describe your dish. Convince them. Lie if you dare."

Pros

  • EVERYONE performs — no passive players
  • The AI moderator is the unique differentiator
  • Simple rules, deep social play
  • Lying about food is natural — everyone does it

Cons

  • Harder to score — how do you judge "best liar"?
  • Describing dishes verbally might feel awkward at first
