## TRACK 1: HEALTH IMPACT
*Build an AI-supported product that tackles a real health challenge in the UK*

This track is about creating solutions that are practical, thoughtful and capable of improving how people experience health, care and prevention in everyday life.

Teams may choose one of the following challenge directions.

### Challenge 1: Student Mental Health Support

Many university students struggle to access timely mental health support, while universities continue to face budget and capacity pressures. Some students actively seek help, but many suffer in silence and never reach support early enough.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Design an AI-supported solution that could be rolled out across universities to improve how students are identified, supported and guided to the right help.

Possible directions include:
- Triage and wellbeing check-ins
- NHS or university-service signposting
- Peer-support coordination
- Earlier identification of students who may be struggling but are not yet asking for help

Strong solutions will work alongside existing services rather than replace clinical care. The best submissions will show a strong understanding of both the student experience and the practical constraints universities face.

### Challenge 2: Making NHS Admin Easier for Patients

For many patients, navigating NHS admin can be confusing, fragmented and stressful. Letters, appointments, referrals, forms, app notifications and follow-up instructions often feel inconsistent and hard to understand, especially for people already dealing with illness or uncertainty.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Design an AI-supported solution that helps patients understand, organise and act on the information they receive from the NHS.

Possible directions include:
- Preparing for appointments
- Summarising concerns before seeing a clinician
- Understanding clinic letters
- Completing forms
- Navigating referrals
- Knowing what to do while waiting for care

The strongest solutions will improve the patient experience while also reducing avoidable friction for NHS staff who currently have to explain, repeat and repair unclear processes.

### Challenge 3: Prevention and Early Intervention

Many preventable health problems are only addressed once symptoms have worsened. The NHS wants to shift towards prevention and earlier action, but in practice many people delay seeking help, struggle to interpret symptoms or do not know what realistic next steps to take.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Build an AI-supported tool that helps people act earlier on their health.

Possible directions include:
- Risk understanding
- Symptom tracking
- Screening
- Community-care navigation
- Turning health data into practical next steps

Teams should focus on a specific preventable issue or intervention point and think carefully about why people delay action. The best solutions will avoid generic lifestyle advice and instead support realistic, specific action without creating unnecessary anxiety.

### Challenge 4: Safer Maternity Care for High-Risk and Under-Heard Mothers

Pregnancy, birth and the postnatal period can involve fast-changing risks, but not all mothers are equally likely to be heard, understood or taken seriously when they raise concerns. In the UK, maternity safety is also shaped by inequality, including differences linked to ethnicity, language, communication barriers and access to care.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Design an AI-supported solution that helps pregnant people and new parents recognise concerns, explain symptoms clearly and find the right route to support.

Possible directions include:
- Plain-language explanations
- Multilingual support
- Appointment preparation
- Care navigation
- Symptom summaries for communication with midwives, GPs or other care teams

Strong solutions should support communication and navigation, not diagnose users or replace clinicians. The best submissions will show sensitivity to safety, inequality and trust.

### Health Impact Submission Requirements

- Build a working prototype or demo within the hackathon period.
- Clearly explain the health problem being addressed.
- Show who the user is and why the problem matters.
- Explain how AI is being used in the product.
- Show why the solution is realistic, useful and different from what already exists.

### Health Impact Judging Focus

- A clear and important health problem
- Strong understanding of the user group
- Thoughtful and appropriate use of AI
- Practical value in a real-world setting
- Clarity and quality of the demo
- Awareness of safety, trust and implementation limits

**HEALTH-TRACK PRINCIPLE:** AI should support users, professionals and existing services. It must not be presented as a replacement for clinical diagnosis, treatment or professional judgement.

## TRACK 2: VIBE WITH ZYMIX
*Build an AI-native feature or mini-product for a Gen Z social app*

Teams should download the app, experience it as real users, identify a genuine opportunity and ship a working prototype during the hackathon.

Teams may choose one of the following challenge directions.

### Challenge 1: Social Vibe Tool

Can you use AI to help two strangers break the ice, or turn an ordinary conversation into something memorable?

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Build something that improves the quality of connection. Do not simply add more buttons; create an interaction that makes people actually feel something.

Possible directions include:
- AI-powered conversation starters
- Real-time emotional-response tools
- Personalised social-style assistants
- Interaction mechanics that make people want to come back

The strongest solutions will show how AI creates a more natural, memorable or emotionally engaging interaction.

### Challenge 2: Entertainment Mini

It does not need to be a full game. It just needs to be something people want to play again the moment it ends.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Create lightweight, social and instantly playable entertainment where AI is what makes the experience genuinely fun.

Possible directions include:
- AI-driven roleplay games between friends
- Real-time quizzes or challenges
- AI-generated co-creation experiences
- Tools that make any group chat instantly more alive

The strongest solutions will be easy to understand, quick to start and enjoyable to repeat.

### Challenge 3: Coordination Helper

Forty messages in the group chat and still no decision made - this is one of the most universal pain points in Gen Z social life.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Use AI to simplify group decision-making and reduce the friction of getting people together.

Possible directions include:
- AI-assisted time and place decision tools
- One-tap event creation with smart reminders
- Multi-person preference aggregation and recommendations
- Tools that finally answer: "Where should we eat tonight?"

The strongest solutions will reduce discussion fatigue and help groups move quickly from conversation to action.

### Challenge 4: Community Builder

Not the people an algorithm pushes at users - the people they actually want to know.

**YOUR CHALLENGE:** Use AI to help Zymix users discover genuinely like-minded communities and turn those connections into something real.

Possible directions include:
- Interest- and behaviour-based intelligent matching
- Tools for building a small community from scratch
- Mechanisms that bring out quieter members
- AI-driven onboarding or community-activation tools

The strongest solutions will demonstrate how AI supports genuine belonging rather than superficial engagement.

### Vibe with Zymix Submission Requirements

- Download and register on the Zymix app before the event.
- Confirm that the app is available in your region.
- Experience the product as real users and identify a genuine opportunity before building.
- Use any suitable AI development tools, such as Cursor, Lovable, Bolt, Figma AI, Claude or others.
- Submit a live demo, a short product brief, an explanation of why the idea matters for Zymix users, and a description of which AI tools were used and how.

### Vibe with Zymix Judging Focus

- Relevance to Zymix users
- Originality of the concept
- Quality of the user experience
- Clarity and polish of the demo
- Effective use of AI

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND TOOL SHOWCASES
*Ownership, public submissions and featured user cases*

### Project Ownership

**TEAMS RETAIN OWNERSHIP:** Teams retain ownership of their projects. Sponsors do not automatically receive ownership or commercial usage rights.

### Public Submission and Sharing

Devpost and GitHub submissions are public or open-source-oriented. Teams should therefore treat anything they submit as material that may be publicly visible, accessed or shared.

- Do not submit confidential, proprietary or commercially sensitive intellectual property.
- Only submit code, content, data, models, media and other materials that your team has the right to share.
- Do not include third-party confidential information, restricted datasets, private credentials or materials that breach another person's rights.
- Check the terms and visibility settings of the platforms and tools you use.

### Work Created During the Hackathon

Projects should be substantially built during the official hackathon period. Teams may use existing tools, libraries, APIs, templates and lawful pre-existing components, but must represent their work honestly and clearly explain what was created during the event.

### AI Tool User-Case Showcases

If your team uses AI tools provided or supported through the event - including Cursor, Manus, Fotor or GLM / Z.ai - your project or workflow may be selected as a featured user case, best-use-case example, event recap or community showcase.

**OWNERSHIP REMAINS WITH THE TEAM:** Being selected for a user-case showcase does not transfer ownership of the project. The team continues to own its work, subject to the public submission settings and platform terms it has accepted.

## 9. YOUR 3-MINUTE DEMO
*Help judges understand the problem, see the product and remember why it matters*

Your goal is not to explain every technical detail. Your goal is to help judges understand the problem, see the product working and remember why it matters.

| Timing | Focus | What to cover |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:25 | Problem and user | Who is this for, and what specific problem are you solving? |
| 0:25-0:50 | Core idea | What did you build, and why is it different or useful? |
| 0:50-2:15 | Live product demo | Show the working product as early as possible. Focus on the main user journey. |
| 2:15-2:40 | AI and workflow | Explain what AI does, why it is needed and which tools materially supported the build. |
| 2:40-3:00 | Impact and close | State the practical value, evidence of completion and the next logical step. |

### Demo Tips

- Open with the user problem, not a long team introduction.
- Show the working product within the first minute.
- Use one strong user journey rather than many unfinished features.
- Prepare seeded data, test accounts and tabs before judges arrive.
- Explain failure modes or limitations honestly when relevant.
- Finish on time and leave space for the judge's question.
